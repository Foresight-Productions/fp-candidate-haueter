Why I'm Running

A prosecutor who knows Yakima County's courtrooms.

Tyler Haueter has served as a Deputy Prosecuting Attorney in the Yakima County Prosecutor's Office, working in the Special Assault Unit on some of the most serious felony cases the office handles. He earned his law degree at the University of Idaho College of Law and has spent his career holding the line for victims of violent crime.

With longtime Prosecutor Joe Brusic stepping aside in 2026, this is a defining moment for Yakima County. Tyler is running to bring proven courtroom experience, a steady hand, and a clear-eyed commitment to public safety to the office of Prosecuting Attorney.

"Crime victims deserve a prosecutor who has actually stood next to them in court. That's the experience I'm bringing to this office."
Priorities

Five commitments to Yakima County.

A prosecutor's first job is to keep our community safe and to stand with the people who have been harmed. Here is how Tyler will lead.

01

Hold Violent Offenders Accountable

Repeat and violent offenders should face the full weight of the law. Tyler will prioritize charging decisions and sentencing recommendations that reflect the seriousness of violent crime in Yakima County.

02

Stand With Crime Victims

Victims and families deserve more than a case number. Tyler will keep the Special Assault Unit and Victim/Witness services strong, and treat every survivor with the respect they have earned.

03

Restore Public Safety

Our families deserve neighborhoods, schools, and businesses where they feel safe. Tyler will work alongside the Sheriff, local police, and community partners to focus prosecution where it matters most.

04

Stand With Law Enforcement

Officers and deputies put themselves on the line every day. Tyler will be a partner who backs sound police work and prosecutes the cases they bring with the urgency and professionalism the job demands.

05

Transparent, By-the-Book Prosecution

Yakima County deserves a Prosecutor's Office that is fair, ethical, and consistent. Tyler will run an office where charging decisions are principled, communication with victims is clear, and the public can trust the work being done in their name.
